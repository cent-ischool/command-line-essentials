Lesson 1: What Is the Terminal?

The terminal is a text-based way to control your computer. It might look intimidating at first, but it's incredibly powerful — and you'll be comfortable with it by the end of this lesson.

Opening the Terminal

Press Win + S, type PowerShell, and hit Enter. Or press Win + R, type powershell, and press Enter.

Press ⌘ + Space, type Terminal, and hit Enter. Or open Finder → Applications → Utilities → Terminal.

Press Ctrl + Alt + T to open the terminal directly. Or search for "Terminal" in your applications menu.

Understanding the Prompt

When PowerShell is ready, you'll see something like: PS C:\Users\YourName> — this means the terminal is waiting for your command.

When Terminal is ready, you'll see something like: YourName@Mac ~ % — the ~ means your home folder.

When the terminal is ready, you'll see something like: user@computer:~$ — the ~ means your home folder and $ means you're a regular user.

Everything after the prompt is where you type. Press Enter to run a command.

Your First Commands

The echo command prints text to the screen. Try it:

echo "Hello, World!"

echo "Hello, World!"

To see your username:

echo $env:USERNAME — prints your Windows username.

whoami — prints your username.

To clear the terminal screen:

cls

clear

Tip: Press ↑ to cycle through previous commands. Press Ctrl+C to cancel a running command.
